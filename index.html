Biography

Virat Kohli is an Indian international cricketer and former captain of the Indian national cricket team. Widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport, Kohli has broken numerous records across formats. Known for his consistency, fitness, and aggression, he has redefined modern-day cricket.

Born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi, India, Kohli rose through the ranks by leading India to victory in the Under-19 World Cup in 2008. He made his international debut the same year and soon established himself as a mainstay in the Indian batting lineup.

Kohli has captained India in all three formats, leading the team to historic Test series wins abroad and achieving the number one ranking in Tests. In the Indian Premier League (IPL), he has been a long-time player and former captain of the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB).

Beyond cricket, Kohli is admired for his discipline, fitness culture, and charitable initiatives. He is an inspiration to millions, representing passion, determination, and the relentless pursuit of excellence.
